Kimetsu no Yaeba (Demon Slayer)

Title: Demon Slayer (Kimetsu no Yaiba)
Adapted From: Manga written by Koyoharu Gotouge
Production Studio: Ufotable
Debut: April 2019
Setting: Taisho-era Japan
Plot Summary: Follows Tanjiro Kamado's journey as a demon slayer after his family's massacre and his sister's transformation into a demon. He joins the Demon Slayer Corps, aiming to cure his sister and avenge his family, while uncovering the secrets of demons.
Episode Duration: Approximately 22-24 minutes per episode Season 1 Air Dates: April to September 2019 (26 episodes) Movie Sequel: "Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba the Movie: Mugen Train" released in October 2020 in Japan.
Highlights: Renowned for stunning animation, compelling storytelling, and character development, the series has gained immense popularity, becoming a significant cultural phenomenon in the anime world.

Boku no Hero Academia (My Hero Academia)

Title: My Hero Academia (Boku no Hero Academia)
Adapted From: Manga written by Kohei Horikoshi
Production Studio: BONES
Plot Summary: In a world where almost everyone has superpowers known as "Quirks," a quirkless boy named Izuku Midoriya idolizes heroes and dreams of becoming one. All Might, the greatest hero, shares his powers with Izuku, granting him a quirk and admission to U.A. High School, a prestigious institution for aspiring heroes. Izuku navigates challenges, forming friendships, and combating villains while learning to control his newfound abilities and striving to become the greatest hero.
Debut: April 2016 Setting: A society where quirks are common, focusing on U.A. High School and the hero society Episode Duration: Approximately 23-24 minutes per episode
Air Dates: Season 1: April to June 2016 (13 episodes)
Season 2: April to September 2017 (25 episodes)
Season 3: April to September 2018 (25 episodes)
Season 4: October 2019 to April 2020 (25 episodes)
Season 5: March to September 2021 (25 episodes)
Highlights: Recognized for its character development, action-packed sequences, and exploration of ethical dilemmas, "My Hero Academia" has gained widespread acclaim, appealing to audiences for its dynamic storytelling and the growth of its protagonist within a vibrant hero-centric world.

Jojo's Bizarre Adventure (JoJo no Kimyou na Bouken)

Title: Jojo's Bizarre Adventure (JoJo no Kimyou na Bouken)
Adapted From: Manga written by Hirohiko Araki
Production Studio: David Production
Plot Summary: Spanning multiple generations of the Joestar family, each part follows a different member known as "Jojo" as they face off against various supernatural foes, often involving mystical artifacts called Stand. These Stands are psychic manifestations that grant their users unique abilities. The series explores themes of honor, family, and the battle between good and evil across different timelines and settings.
Debut: The manga began in 1987 and has multiple story arcs.
Setting: The story spans different time periods and locations, each focusing on a different member of the Joestar family.
Episode Duration: Approximately 20-25 minutes per episode, varying across different arcs.
Air Dates: The anime adaptation is divided into various seasons, each corresponding to different story arcs. Some of the seasons and their airing dates include:
"Jojo's Bizarre Adventure: Phantom Blood" (Part 1) in 2012
"Jojo's Bizarre Adventure: Battle Tendency" (Part 2) in 2012-2013
"Jojo's Bizarre Adventure: Stardust Crusaders" (Part 3) in 2014-2015
"Jojo's Bizarre Adventure: Diamond is Unbreakable" (Part 4) in 2016
"Jojo's Bizarre Adventure: Golden Wind" (Part 5) in 2018-2019
"Jojo's Bizarre Adventure: Stone Ocean" (Part 6) announced for future release
Highlights: Known for its unique art style, flamboyant characters, and creative storytelling, "Jojo's Bizarre Adventure" has earned a dedicated fan base and critical acclaim for its inventive approach to action and the supernatural.
